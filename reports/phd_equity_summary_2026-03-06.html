Panel structure and scope

  • Rows: 54 Regina CT rows (official StatsCan covariates, inferred lead/replacement allocations normalized to city totals).
  • Replacement metrics: active lead count, strict replaced (2019+ constrained), replacement rate vs active lead.
  • Socioeconomic covariates: children age bands, tenure, income/LIM, immigrant/visible minority, lone-parent, unemployment, education, Indigenous identity.

Lead/replacement fields are inferred allocations for tract-level diagnostics; not direct connection-level event logs.

Method caveats (important)

  • Sample size is small (n=14 DA-proxy areas), so inference is sensitive to outliers and model specification.
  • Proxy geography can induce ecological bias; household-level tenure/demographic effects are unresolved in this run.
  • Regression is descriptive and used as a robustness check only.
  • No claim of causal equity performance is made from these results.

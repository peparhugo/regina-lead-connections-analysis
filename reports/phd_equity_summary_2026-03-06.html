Regina Lead Connections — Equity & Prioritization Research Brief

Date: 2026-03-06 • Status: Phase 1 quantitative baseline (preliminary, reproducible)

Executive summary

  • We now publish two GIS metrics: Strict inferred replacements (Pb→non-Pb only) and Broad inferred activity (includes lead records with update changes).
  • Current strict inferred total is 1,349, broadly consistent in scale with city-reported progress context.
  • At area-level, strict inferred replacement rate is positively associated with lead burden (higher lead% areas show higher replacement rates in this snapshot).
  • This does not prove causal fairness; it is an observational signal from public layer states.

What we tested today

  • Joined strict inferred replacements by area to City LeadConnectionAreas burden fields.
  • Computed area metric: strict_replacements / current_lead_total.
  • Assessed relationship against area lead burden (Lead_Per).

Results (n=14 areas):
Pearson r ≈ 0.574
Spearman ρ ≈ 0.714

Interpretation: in this snapshot, replacement activity appears concentrated more in higher-burden areas, not lower-burden areas.

Priority concerns requested by Pepar (active workstream)

  • Renter vs homeowner patterns
  • Demographic differences (including Indigenous identity indicators where available at aggregate geography)
  • Income gradients and potential affluent-area priority effects
  • Program structure confounders (city-owned vs private-side replacement pathways)

Data limitations (current)

  • No direct owner/renter flag in the public lead service line layer used for strict counts.
  • No person-level demographic data should be used; all equity work must stay at aggregate census geography.
  • City-reported annual totals are the official program metric; GIS-derived counts are analytic estimates.

Next research steps (P0)

  1. Build CT/DA panel with replacement intensity + census covariates (tenure, income, low-income, Indigenous identity where public aggregate tables permit).
  2. Run significance tests and model checks (rank tests + robust regression with uncertainty notes).
  3. Publish methods + reproducibility appendix and a plain-language equity summary page.

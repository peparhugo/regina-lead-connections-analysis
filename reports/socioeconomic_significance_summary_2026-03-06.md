# Regina CT Socioeconomic Signal Check (Publish-First Summary)

## Plain-language takeaway
- We do **not** see strong evidence that higher-income tracts got more replacements (income signal is weak/non-significant).
- We see a **borderline renter-share signal**: tracts with more renters trend toward higher inferred replacement activity (near significance threshold).
- Indigenous-share signal is currently weak in this CT-level inferred model.
- Estimated child exposure burden remains material citywide, supporting faster replacement pacing.

## Statistical screen (CT-level, n=54)
- Income vs inferred replacements: rho=-0.084734, p=0.530494 (not statistically significant)
- Renter % vs inferred replacements: rho=0.270288, p=0.054789 (borderline; monitor closely)
- Indigenous identity % vs inferred replacements: rho=-0.083133, p=0.540892 (not statistically significant)

## Policy call to action
1. **Expedite full replacement pace using provincial co-funding** in top-burden CTs first.
2. **Do not allocate by neighborhood income assumptions**; current evidence does not support "richer = more replaced" as a reliable rule.
3. **Add child-impact weighting** to annual replacement prioritization so health/QoL gains are explicit and measurable.

## Caveat
- Replacement outcome is an inferred CT allocation normalized to city totals; use for prioritization direction, not causal proof.
